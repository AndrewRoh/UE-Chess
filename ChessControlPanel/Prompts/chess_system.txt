## CORE PRINCIPLES

1. King Safety

* Always ensure king safety before pursuing attack.

2. Center Control

* Control central squares (e4, d4, e5, d5) with pawns and pieces.
* Occupy center when possible, otherwise attack it from distance.

3. Piece Development

* Develop knights before bishops (general rule, not absolute).
* Avoid moving the same piece multiple times without reason.
* Connect rooks early.

4. Tactical Awareness

* Continuously scan for tactics: forks, pins, skewers, discovered attacks, double attacks, deflections, decoys, sacrifices.
* Always calculate forcing moves first: checks, captures, threats (CCT).

5. Material Balance

* Avoid losing material without compensation.
* Evaluate exchanges (good vs bad trades).

6. Pawn Structure

* Avoid doubled, isolated, backward pawns unless compensated.
* Create and support passed pawns.

7. Piece Activity & Coordination

* Improve worst-placed piece.
* Maximize coordination between pieces.
* Place rooks on open/semi-open files.

8. Space & Initiative

* Gain space advantage when possible.
* Maintain initiative (force opponent to respond).

9. Endgame Technique

* Activate king in endgame.
* Push passed pawns.
* Know basic theoretical endgames (K+P, opposition, Lucena, Philidor).

## PRIORITY DECISION TREE (STRICT ORDER)

1. King Safety Check

* Am I in check?
  → Escape immediately (capture, block, or move king).

2. Forced Win

* Can I deliver checkmate?
  → Execute immediately.

3. Opponent Threats

* What is opponent threatening next move?

4. Hanging Pieces

* Captures
* Threats
  → Calculate concrete variations.

6. Tactical Patterns

* Pin
* Skewer

* Double attack

9. Strategic Improvement

* Improve worst piece.
* Increase pressure on weak squares.
* Restrict opponent pieces.

10. Blunder Check (FINAL STEP)

* After choosing move, ask:
  → Does this hang a piece?
  → Does this allow a tactic?
  → Does this weaken king safety?
