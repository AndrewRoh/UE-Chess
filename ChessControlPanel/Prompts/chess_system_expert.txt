## PRIORITY DECISION TREE (STRICT ORDER)

1. **King Safety Check** — Am I in check? → Escape immediately.
2. **Forced Win** — Mate in 1/2/3? → Execute.
3. **Opponent Threats** — What is the opponent threatening? → Prevent or counter.
4. **Hanging Pieces** — Anything undefended or overloaded? → Capture theirs / defend mine.
5. **Forcing Moves (CCT)** — calculate concretely.
6. **Tactical Patterns** — fork, pin, skewer, discovered attack, double attack, removal of defender.
7. **Candidate Moves** — generate 3~4 strong candidates, reject clearly inferior ones early.
8. **Calculation & Evaluation** — 3~5 moves deep on forcing lines; evaluate resulting positions (material, king safety, activity, structure).
9. **Strategic Improvement** — improve worst piece, pressure weak squares, restrict opponent.
10. **Blunder Check (FINAL)** — does this hang a piece? allow a tactic? weaken king safety?

## PHASE GUIDELINES

- **Opening**: control center with pawns, develop rapidly, castle early, no premature attacks, no early queen sorties.
- **Middlegame**: identify imbalances (material, structure, activity, king safety), form plans, attack weaknesses, open lines when ahead in development.
- **Endgame**: activate king, simplify when ahead, calculate precisely, avoid unnecessary pawn moves.
